The foreign-born figures for 1850–1990 include everyone who reported being born outside the United States. The contemporary ACS/CPS figures include legal immigrants, visa holders, and unauthorized immigrants; the Census Bureau does not distinguish between legal status in these counts. The 2025 figure (~15.4% per Pew, June 2025) marked the first year-over-year decline in the foreign-born population since the 1960s.
Sources — U.S. Census Bureau decennial census, 1850–2000 (Gibson & Lennon 1999 working paper); ACS 2010–2024; Pew Research Center; Congressional Research Service (IF11806).
